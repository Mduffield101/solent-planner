Key implication for the Solent gates: the two passages that hinge on tide are Hurst Narrows (Day 2) and the Chichester Bar (Days 3–4). Both are easier later in the week as the streams ease (≈60%→45% of springs) — but on Days 1–2 (the week's strongest tides) the western-Solent streams still run briskly, so timing matters most early on.

Tidal Planning Notes

ℹ️

Short shakedown hop. Leaving Portsmouth Harbour, observe QHM procedures and the small-boat channel — keep clear of commercial and naval traffic in the harbour entrance and the Solent precautionary area.

Cross NW to the mouth of Southampton Water and up to the Hamble. A short leg, so tide timing is non-critical, but a fair west-going stream in the early afternoon will help.

✅

Hamble entry: well-marked and accessible at most states; the river runs a brisk stream, so approach the marina pontoons with that in mind. MDL Hamble Point — booked ×2 (£60/boat).

ℹ️

Southampton Water: benefits from the famous young flood stand / prolonged HW — a generous arrival window.

Hurst & Yarmouth (W Solent) run ~5–10 min ahead of Portsmouth. Plan to carry the ebb (W-going) down to Hurst — broadly after the morning HW. Confirm on EasyTide.

Tidal Planning Notes

⚠️

Hurst Narrows: the western Solent constricts between Hurst Point and the Island. Streams here run hard — up to ~4 kn at springs. Carry the west-going (ebb) stream down the Solent; avoid arriving against a strong wind-over-tide, which kicks up a short, steep sea off Hurst.

Plan to pass Hurst with a fair tide — broadly on the ebb (after local HW). With easing springs this will still add 2–3 kn to your speed over ground.

✅

Yarmouth entry: straightforward and accessible on any tide; well-marked approach. A comfortable 21 nm day.

Chichester Bar — Tidal Gate ⚠

⚠️

Chichester Bar is the critical gate of the week. The bar is shallow (charted ~0.7m, less at points) and dangerous in strong onshore winds against the ebb. Do not cross near LW, especially at springs.

Aim to cross the bar on a rising tide, ideally within roughly HW Chichester −3h to +1h, in fair conditions. The Bar Beacon marks the approach — follow the recommended track and the harbour entrance buoyage.

ℹ️

This is the long leg (~30 nm) and routing is east the full length of the Solent — carry the east-going flood. A mid-morning departure from Yarmouth lets you ride the flood east and arrive at Chichester on the rise. Build in the mainland lunch stop (DCA drop-off) around Portsmouth/Gosport en route.

ℹ️

Strong streams run in the harbour entrance (up to ~5 kn springs in the narrows by the Winner). Plan the bar timing first, then back-calculate the Yarmouth departure.

Tidal Planning Notes

⚠️

Chichester Bar (exit): same gate as Day 3 — leave with sufficient height of tide over the bar, ideally on the rise or near HW; avoid the ebb against fresh onshore wind.

ℹ️

Once clear of the bar, take the west-going stream back along the Solent to Cowes. Near-neap streams make this an easier passage than earlier in the week.

⚠️

Cowes / River Medina: busy entrance — watch for the chain ferry (floating bridge) across the river and strong ebb out of the Medina. Follow the small-craft channel. West Cowes Yacht Haven — booked ×2 (Ch 80, £50/boat + £5 electric).
